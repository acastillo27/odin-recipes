Chicken Adobo Recipe

Ingredients

  • Chicken Thighs: Typically 4-6 pieces, depending on their size
  • Soy Sauce: About 1/2 cup (120ml) or to taste
  • Vinegar: Approximately 1/2 cup (120ml) or to taste. You can use white vinegar or cane vinegar
  • Garlic cloves (minced): Around 4-6 cloves, depending on your preference
  • Onion (sliced): One medium-sized onion or half a large onion, sliced
  • Bay Leaves: 2-3 leaves
  • Peppercorns: About 1 teaspoon
  • Brown Sugar (Optional): 1-2 tablespoons, adjust to your desired sweetness
  • Green Onion (for garnish): A few sprigs, chopped, for garnishing the dish

Instructions

  1. In a large bowl or resealable bag, combine the soy sauce, vinegar, minced garlic, sliced onion, bay leaves, whole peppercorns, and sugar. Mix well to combine the marinade.
  2. Add the chicken thighs to the marinade and make sure they are coated evenly. Marinate for at least 30 minutes or refrigerate overnight for better flavor.
  3. Heat a large pan or pot over medium-high heat. Remove the chicken thighs from the marinade and set aside. Reserve the marinade for later.
  4. In the same pan, sear the chicken thighs skin-side down until browned and crispy, about 4-5 minutes. Flip the chicken thighs and brown the other side for another 4-5 minutes.
  5. Pour the reserved marinade into the pan with the chicken thighs. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat to low and cover the pan. Let it simmer for about 30-40 minutes or until the chicken is tender and cooked through.
  6. Remove the lid and increase the heat to medium-high. Allow the sauce to reduce and thicken for about 5-10 minutes.
  7. Once the sauce has thickened, remove the bay leaves. Taste and adjust the seasoning if needed.
  8. Serve the Filipino chicken adobo hot over steamed rice. Garnish with chopped green onions. Enjoy!
